Tag

A compact label whose semantic colour carries meaning — a status, a category, a marker. One component (the styleguide's Eff Tag): pick a background and a radius. There is no separate “badge”.

Anatomy

A small filled label. Its colour carries the meaning; the radius sets whether it reads as a compact marker or a rounded pill.

Completed
1
Surface — A filled label. The background is the tag's whole point: it maps to one meaning (info, brand, warning, negative, highlight, positive).
2
Icon — Optional leading icon (12px), inheriting the label colour.
3
Label — A short word or two. The tag never wraps.
4
Radius — Medium (default) for a compact marker, or full for a pill.

Usage

One component covers what other systems split into “badge” and “tag”. Use the background for meaning and the radius for the shape you want.

Variants

Two independent properties: the semantic background and the radius. Combine freely.

Background

Info
Info (default) — A neutral-but-notable fact worth surfacing. Blue reads as “for your information”, not success or warning.
Brand
Brand — A lightly branded marker, e.g. a selected or primary label.
Warning
Warning — Something needs attention or is nearing a limit. Orange draws the eye without reading as an error.
Negative
Negative — A problem, failure or blocking state. Reserve red for genuinely negative meaning.
Highlight
Highlight — In progress or noteworthy right now (a survey collecting responses). Yellow catches the eye; text stays dark for contrast.
Positive
Positive — A successful, finished outcome. Green is the strongest “all good” signal.

Radius

Medium
Medium (default) — Softly rounded corners. The compact, status-marker look — this is what a “badge” is elsewhere.
Full
Full (.tag-full) — A fully-rounded pill. Use for a softer, more “label-y” feel, e.g. categories and metadata.

Content guidelines

A tag has room for only a few words — make each one count.

Name the meaning in one or two words

The label should be readable at a glance. Long phrases wrap or truncate and defeat the purpose.

Completed
Do
A single clear word, optionally with a short date.
This survey finished collecting responses
Don't
Pack a whole sentence into the tag.

Accessibility

Colour carries the meaning, so the text has to carry it too.

→
Never colour-only. The label states the meaning in words. A user who can't tell green from yellow still reads “Completed” vs “Running”.
→
Enough contrast. White on the dark fills, ink on the yellow highlight — keep the label legible on its fill.
→
Not a control. A tag is text, not focusable. If it must be clicked or dismissed, it's the wrong component — use a Button or a filter control.
→
Announce live changes. If a tag updates in place, put it (or its container) in a polite live region.

Emphasise the status, lighter detail

Give the tag a lighter weight (.text-w400 — the styleguide's [weight]) and wrap the status word in <b>, so the state reads first and a trailing date/detail sits back.

Completed on Jul 7, 2026 Running until Jul 17, 2026 
<span class="tag tag-positive text-w400"><b>Completed</b> on Jul 7, 2026</span>
⚠️
One component (Eff Tag) The Angular styleguide ships a single Eff Tag: [background] = info (default) / brand / warning / negative / highlight / positive, [radius] = medium (default) / full, plus [textSize] and [weight]. There is no separate “badge” — this page's classes mirror that one component. Survey/report status pills have their own higher-level Survey Status component (date-driven); don't hand-roll them from a Tag in production.

Accessibility

Keep the meaning in the text, not just the colour.

→
Text-first. The word is always present; colour only reinforces it.
→
Not focusable. A tag is a <span> — no tab stop, no click handler.
→
Live updates. If it changes in place, wrap it in aria-live="polite".
